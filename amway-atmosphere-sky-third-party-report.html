Amway Atmosphere Sky Air Purifier

Third-Party Scientific Test Report Summary

Prepared: 2026-05-25 Scope: English-language sources only

Executive Summary

This review searched for English-language third-party sources covering the Amway Atmosphere Sky air purifier, with emphasis on independently verified performance data such as CADR, particle-removal efficiency, and noise testing.

Main finding: publicly accessible third-party data is strongest for CADR, via the AHAM Verifide directory. Evidence for filtration efficiency is widely quoted in English-language materials, but a publicly accessible original third-party laboratory report could not be verified during this search. No independent English-language public noise test report with clear methodology and measured dB results was confirmed.

Research Method

The search prioritized the following source types:

1. Independent certification databases

2. Third-party laboratory or certification bodies

3. Publicly accessible English-language test summaries or certification pages

4. Public manufacturer claims only when needed for context, not as primary evidence

Non-English pages, dealer pages, and duplicated marketing claims were excluded from the evidence summary unless necessary to explain the absence of third-party data.

2) Ozone-related third-party certification framework

Source: Intertek Zero Ozone Certification Program https://www.intertek.com/sustainability/zero-ozone/

Intertek documents an independent certification program for air-cleaning devices with an ozone emission threshold of no more than 0.005 ppm (5 ppb).

Relevant published points from Intertek:

  • Independent third-party certification for ultra-low ozone emissions
  • Certification threshold: 0.005 ppm (5 ppb)
  • Positioned as stricter than FDA and CARB limits of 0.05 ppm (50 ppb)
  • Annual renewal required
  • Reverification testing required every 3 years

Important limitation:

This source confirms the third-party certification framework and threshold, but this search did not verify a publicly accessible Atmosphere Sky product-specific certificate page directly from Intertek. Therefore, this source should be used to describe the standard itself, not as final proof that a specific Atmosphere Sky unit currently holds the certification unless a product-specific listing is separately confirmed.

3) Filtration efficiency: widely quoted, but public third-party original report not confirmed

Common English-language claim encountered in marketplace materials:

"Removes 99.99% of particles as small as 0.007 microns."

Assessment:

  • This claim is widely repeated in English-language product and distributor materials.
  • However, during this search, no publicly accessible original third-party laboratory report, public certification database entry, or downloadable English test report was confirmed that documents the full test method, aerosol type, face velocity, sampling method, or pass/fail protocol behind this exact figure.

Evidence conclusion:

This filtration-efficiency figure may be repeated in English, but it should not be presented as a fully verified public third-party scientific result unless the original lab report is obtained.

Recommended citation language:

"English-language materials widely cite a filtration claim of 99.99% removal of particles as small as 0.007 microns, but a publicly accessible original third-party laboratory report was not confirmed in this review."

4) Noise testing: no confirmed public independent English report found

Target sought:

A credible independent English-language noise test should ideally include:

  • measured sound pressure levels in dB(A)
  • test distance
  • fan-speed setting(s)
  • room/background noise conditions
  • instrument or methodology description

Result:

No public independent English-language noise test report meeting those standards was confirmed during this review.

Evidence conclusion:

Any noise figures found only in product specifications or dealer pages should be treated as manufacturer specifications rather than independent third-party scientific tests.

Recommended citation language:

"No publicly located independent English-language noise test report for the Amway Atmosphere Sky was confirmed in this review."

Bottom-Line Conclusion

Based on publicly accessible English-language evidence, the Amway Atmosphere Sky has credible third-party CADR validation through AHAM Verifide, and this is the strongest performance data available for citation.

For filtration efficiency, a commonly repeated English-language claim exists, but the original public third-party test report was not confirmed in this review. For noise performance, no public independent English-language test report with verifiable methodology was confirmed.

Accordingly, the most defensible evidence-based summary is:

1. CADR: supported by AHAM Verifide third-party data

2. Filtration efficiency: often quoted, but public third-party original report not confirmed

3. Noise: no confirmed independent English public test report found
